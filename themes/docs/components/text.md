		<pre style="word-wrap: break-word; white-space: pre-wrap;"># Text  Foundational text primitive.  ```jsx &lt;Text&gt;The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.&lt;/Text&gt; ```  ## [API Reference](#api-reference)  This component is based on the `span` element and supports [common margin props](/themes/docs/overview/layout#margin-props).  | Prop           | Type                                                      | Default          | | -------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------- | | `as`           | `"span" \| "div" \| "label" \| "p"`                       | `"span"`         | | `asChild`      | `boolean`                                                 | No default value | | `size`         | `Responsive&lt;enum&gt;`                                        | No default value | | `weight`       | `Responsive&lt;"light" \| "regular" \| "medium" \| "bold"&gt;`  | No default value | | `align`        | `Responsive&lt;"left" \| "center" \| "right"&gt;`               | No default value | | `trim`         | `Responsive&lt;"normal" \| "start" \| "end" \| "both"&gt;`      | No default value | | `truncate`     | `boolean`                                                 | No default value | | `wrap`         | `Responsive&lt;"wrap" \| "nowrap" \| "pretty" \| "balance"&gt;` | No default value | | `color`        | `enum`                                                    | No default value | | `highContrast` | `boolean`                                                 | No default value |  ## [Examples](#examples)  ### [As another element](#as-another-element)  Use the `as` prop to render text as a `p`, `label`, `div` or `span`. This prop is purely semantic and does not alter visual appearance.  ```jsx &lt;Text as="p"&gt;This is a &lt;Strong&gt;paragraph&lt;/Strong&gt; element.&lt;/Text&gt;  &lt;Text as="label"&gt;This is a &lt;Strong&gt;label&lt;/Strong&gt; element.&lt;/Text&gt;  &lt;Text as="div"&gt;This is a &lt;Strong&gt;div&lt;/Strong&gt; element.&lt;/Text&gt;  &lt;Text as="span"&gt;This is a &lt;Strong&gt;span&lt;/Strong&gt; element.&lt;/Text&gt; ```  ### [Size](#size)  Use the `size` prop to control text size. This prop also provides correct line height and corrective letter spacing—as text size increases, the relative line height and letter spacing decrease.  ```jsx &lt;Flex direction="column" gap="3"&gt;   &lt;Text size="1"&gt;The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.&lt;/Text&gt;    &lt;Text size="2"&gt;The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.&lt;/Text&gt;    &lt;Text size="3"&gt;The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.&lt;/Text&gt;    &lt;Text size="4"&gt;The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.&lt;/Text&gt;    &lt;Text size="5"&gt;The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.&lt;/Text&gt;    &lt;Text size="6"&gt;The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.&lt;/Text&gt;    &lt;Text size="7"&gt;The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.&lt;/Text&gt;    &lt;Text size="8"&gt;The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.&lt;/Text&gt;    &lt;Text size="9"&gt;The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.&lt;/Text&gt; &lt;/Flex&gt; ```  Sizes 2–4 are designed to work well for long-form content.  ```jsx &lt;Text as="p" mb="5" size="4"&gt;    The goal of typography is to relate font size, line height, and line width in a proportional way that maximizes beauty and makes reading easier and more pleasant. The question is: What proportion(s) will give us the best results? The golden ratio is often observed in nature where beauty and utility intersect; perhaps we can use this “divine” proportion to enhance these attributes in our typography.  &lt;/Text&gt;    &lt;Text as="p" mb="5" size="3"&gt;    The goal of typography is to relate font size, line height, and line width in a proportional way that maximizes beauty and makes reading easier and more pleasant. The question is: What proportion(s) will give us the best results? The golden ratio is often observed in nature where beauty and utility intersect; perhaps we can use this “divine” proportion to enhance these attributes in our typography.  &lt;/Text&gt;    &lt;Text as="p" size="2" color="gray"&gt;    The goal of typography is to relate font size, line height, and line width in a proportional way that maximizes beauty and makes reading easier and more pleasant. The question is: What proportion(s) will give us the best results? The golden ratio is often observed in nature where beauty and utility intersect; perhaps we can use this “divine” proportion to enhance these attributes in our typography.  &lt;/Text&gt; ```  Sizes 1–3 are designed to work well for UI labels.  ```jsx &lt;Grid align="center" columns="2" gap="5" p="3"&gt;   &lt;Flex direction="column"&gt;     &lt;Text size="3" weight="bold"&gt;       Get started     &lt;/Text&gt;      &lt;Text color="gray" size="2"&gt;       Start your next project in minutes     &lt;/Text&gt;   &lt;/Flex&gt;    &lt;Flex direction="column"&gt;     &lt;Text size="2" weight="bold"&gt;       Get started     &lt;/Text&gt;      &lt;Text color="gray" size="2"&gt;       Start your next project in minutes     &lt;/Text&gt;   &lt;/Flex&gt;    &lt;Flex direction="column"&gt;     &lt;Text size="2" weight="bold"&gt;       Get started     &lt;/Text&gt;      &lt;Text color="gray" size="1"&gt;       Start your next project in minutes     &lt;/Text&gt;   &lt;/Flex&gt;    &lt;Flex direction="column"&gt;     &lt;Text size="1" weight="bold"&gt;       Get started     &lt;/Text&gt;      &lt;Text color="gray" size="1"&gt;       Start your next project in minutes     &lt;/Text&gt;   &lt;/Flex&gt; &lt;/Grid&gt; ```  ### [Weight](#weight)  Use the `weight` prop to set the text weight.  ```jsx &lt;Text weight="regular" as="div"&gt;    The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.  &lt;/Text&gt;    &lt;Text weight="medium" as="div"&gt;    The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.  &lt;/Text&gt;    &lt;Text weight="bold" as="div"&gt;    The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.  &lt;/Text&gt; ```  ### [Align](#align)  Use the `align` prop to set text alignment.  ```jsx &lt;Text align="left" as="div"&gt;Left-aligned&lt;/Text&gt;  &lt;Text align="center" as="div"&gt;Center-aligned&lt;/Text&gt;  &lt;Text align="right" as="div"&gt;Right-aligned&lt;/Text&gt; ```  ### [Trim](#trim)  Use the `trim` prop to trim the leading space at the start, end, or both sides of the text box.  The prop works similarly to the upcoming [half-leading control](https://www.w3.org/TR/css-inline-3/#leading-trim) spec, but uses a common [negative margin workaround](https://seek-oss.github.io/capsize/) under the hood for cross-browser support.  ```jsx &lt;Flex direction="column" gap="3"&gt;   &lt;Text     trim="normal"     style={{       background: "var(--gray-a2)",       borderTop: "1px dashed var(--gray-a7)",       borderBottom: "1px dashed var(--gray-a7)",     }}   &gt;     Without trim   &lt;/Text&gt;    &lt;Text     trim="both"     style={{       background: "var(--gray-a2)",       borderTop: "1px dashed var(--gray-a7)",       borderBottom: "1px dashed var(--gray-a7)",     }}   &gt;     With trim   &lt;/Text&gt; &lt;/Flex&gt; ```  Trimming the leading is useful when dialing in vertical spacing in cards or other “boxy” components. Otherwise, padding looks larger on top and bottom than on the sides.  ```jsx &lt;Flex direction="column" gap="3"&gt;   &lt;Box     style={{       background: "var(--gray-a2)",       border: "1px dashed var(--gray-a7)",     }}     p="4"   &gt;     &lt;Heading mb="1" size="3"&gt;       Without trim     &lt;/Heading&gt;      &lt;Text&gt;       The goal of typography is to relate font size, line height, and line width in a proportional       way that maximizes beauty and makes reading easier and more pleasant.     &lt;/Text&gt;   &lt;/Box&gt;    &lt;Box     p="4"     style={{       background: "var(--gray-a2)",       border: "1px dashed var(--gray-a7)",     }}   &gt;     &lt;Heading mb="1" size="3" trim="start"&gt;       With trim     &lt;/Heading&gt;      &lt;Text trim="end"&gt;       The goal of typography is to relate font size, line height, and line width in a proportional       way that maximizes beauty and makes reading easier and more pleasant.     &lt;/Text&gt;   &lt;/Box&gt; &lt;/Flex&gt; ```  The default trim values are configured for the system font stack that’s used by Radix Themes. If you are using custom fonts, you can [adjust](/themes/docs/theme/typography#customization) the trim values using the corresponding CSS variables.  ```css .radix-themes {   --default-leading-trim-start: 0.42em;    --default-leading-trim-end: 0.36em;    --heading-leading-trim-start: 0.42em;    --heading-leading-trim-end: 0.36em; } ```  ### [Truncate](#truncate)  Use the `truncate` prop to truncate text with an ellipsis when it overflows its container.  ```jsx &lt;Flex maxWidth="300px"&gt;   &lt;Text truncate&gt;     The goal of typography is to relate font size, line height, and line width in a proportional way     that maximizes beauty and makes reading easier and more pleasant.   &lt;/Text&gt; &lt;/Flex&gt; ```  ### [Wrap](#wrap)  Use the `wrap` prop to control text wrapping.  ```jsx &lt;Flex maxWidth="270px"&gt;   &lt;Text wrap="nowrap"&gt;     The goal of typography is to relate font size, line height, and line width in a proportional way     that maximizes beauty and makes reading easier and more pleasant.   &lt;/Text&gt; &lt;/Flex&gt; ```  ```jsx &lt;Flex maxWidth="270px"&gt;   &lt;Text wrap="balance"&gt;     The goal of typography is to relate font size, line height, and line width in a proportional way     that maximizes beauty and makes reading easier and more pleasant.   &lt;/Text&gt; &lt;/Flex&gt; ```  ```jsx &lt;Flex maxWidth="270px"&gt;   &lt;Text wrap="pretty"&gt;     The goal of typography is to relate font size, line height, and line width in a proportional way     that maximizes beauty and makes reading easier and more pleasant.   &lt;/Text&gt; &lt;/Flex&gt; ```  `text-wrap: pretty` is an experimental value that is&lt;!-- --&gt; [not yet supported in all browsers](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/text-wrap#browser_compatibility). However, it can be treated as a progressive enhancement for browsers that do support it.  ### [Color](#color)  Use the `color` prop to assign a specific [color](/themes/docs/theme/color). The text colors are designed to achieve at least Lc 60 APCA contrast over common background colors.  ```jsx &lt;Flex direction="column"&gt;   &lt;Text color="indigo"&gt;The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.&lt;/Text&gt;    &lt;Text color="cyan"&gt;The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.&lt;/Text&gt;    &lt;Text color="orange"&gt;The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.&lt;/Text&gt;    &lt;Text color="crimson"&gt;The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.&lt;/Text&gt; &lt;/Flex&gt; ```  ### [High-contrast](#high-contrast)  Use the `highContrast` prop to increase color contrast with the background.  ```jsx &lt;Flex direction="column"&gt;   &lt;Text color="gray"&gt;The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.&lt;/Text&gt;    &lt;Text color="gray" highContrast&gt;     The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.   &lt;/Text&gt; &lt;/Flex&gt; ```  ### [With formatting](#with-formatting)  Compose `Text` with formatting components to add emphasis and structure to content.  ```jsx &lt;Text as="p"&gt;   Look, such a helpful &lt;Link href="#"&gt;link&lt;/Link&gt;, an &lt;Em&gt;italic emphasis&lt;/Em&gt;, a piece of computer{" "}   &lt;Code&gt;code&lt;/Code&gt;, and even a hotkey combination &lt;Kbd&gt;⇧⌘A&lt;/Kbd&gt; within the text. &lt;/Text&gt; ```  ### [With form controls](#with-form-controls)  Composing `Text` with a form control like [`Checkbox`](/themes/docs/components/checkbox), [`RadioGroup`](/themes/docs/components/radio-group), or [`Switch`](/themes/docs/components/switch) automatically centers the control with the first line of text, even when the text is multi-line.  ```jsx &lt;Box maxWidth="300px"&gt;   &lt;Text as="label" size="3"&gt;     &lt;Flex gap="2"&gt;       &lt;Checkbox defaultChecked /&gt; I understand that these documents are confidential and cannot be       shared with a third party.     &lt;/Flex&gt;   &lt;/Text&gt; &lt;/Box&gt; ```
